Frédéric Chopin

Frederic Chopin (1810-1849) was a Polish composer and virtuoso pianist of French-Polish parentage. He arrived in Paris in September 1831, and never returned to his homeland of Poland. He is considered one of the great masters of Romantic music.

Most of Chopin's music was written for the piano. He wrote 59 mazurkas, 27 études, 27 preludes, 21 nocturnes, and 20 waltzes for the instrument. Chopin wrote around 23 polonaises throughout his life. His most famous examples are the Military' Polonaise in A, 'Heroic' or 'Drum' Polonaise in A-flat. Like much of Chopin's music, most of the pieces are written for solo piano, but the Andante spianato et grande polonaise brillante is an orchestral piece.
